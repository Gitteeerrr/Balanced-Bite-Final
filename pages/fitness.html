🏋️ The Science of Movement

How exercise turns your body into an efficient energy-burning machine.

Metabolic Flexibility

Innovation in fitness science has moved beyond just "burning calories." We now focus on Metabolic Flexibility.

This is your body's ability to switch fuels. When you eat, your body burns glucose (sugar). When you exercise, you train your mitochondria (the power plants in your cells) to switch over and burn stored fat for fuel more efficiently.

Why it matters: People who don't exercise often lose this flexibility, meaning their bodies stay in "sugar-burning mode" all the time, making it much harder to lose weight even if they eat less!

The "Afterburn" Effect

Known scientifically as EPOC (Excess Post-exercise Oxygen Consumption).

After a high-intensity workout, your body continues to burn calories at a higher rate for up to 24 hours while it repairs muscles and restores oxygen levels. You are essentially burning your lunch while you're sitting on the couch later!

⚡ Aerobic (Cardio)

Running or swimming improves your heart's ability to deliver oxygen to your muscles. This makes your "engine" more efficient at burning fuel over long periods.

💪 Resistance (Strength)

Muscle is "expensive" tissue. It takes a lot of energy just to keep it on your body. Every pound of muscle you gain increases your Resting Metabolic Rate.
