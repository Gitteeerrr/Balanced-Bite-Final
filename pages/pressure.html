🩺 The Science of Blood Pressure

How your diet controls the pressure in your pipes.

The Sodium-Water Connection

Think of Sodium as a **water magnet**. When you eat high-sodium foods (like chips or canned soup), your body pulls water into your blood vessels to balance the salt levels.

This extra fluid increases the volume of your blood, forcing your heart to pump harder. Over time, this "High Pressure" damages the delicate lining of your arteries, leading to heart disease.

The Potassium Balance

**Potassium** is the "Reliever." It helps your kidneys flush out the extra sodium and relaxes the tension in your blood vessel walls. By eating more potassium (bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach), you are essentially "opening up" the pipes to let blood flow more freely.

⚠️ The Hidden Salt ⚠️

Most people think the salt shaker is the enemy. However, 75% of our salt intake comes from processed foods:

  • Bread & Tortillas
  • Deli Meats (Ham/Turkey)
  • Canned Soups
  • Pizza & Frozen Meals
